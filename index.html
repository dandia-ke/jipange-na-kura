🗳️ Civic Education

How to Vote in Kenya

Everything you need before the polling booth — the 6 ballot papers, how to mark them, and what happens step by step.

Your 6 Ballot Papers

Each election has its own colour — so you always know which paper is which.

1
Presidential Ballot
⬜ White — Form 30
Vote for the President & Deputy President as a pair. Every registered Kenyan votes for the same national candidates.
✓ Mark ONE candidate pair only
2
MP — National Assembly
🟩 Green — Form 26
Vote for your constituency MP. They represent you in Parliament and oversee the NG-CDF fund for local development.
✓ Mark ONE candidate
3
County Women Representative
🟪 Purple — Form 27
Vote for the Women Rep for your whole county. She sits in the National Assembly championing women, youth and persons with disabilities.
✓ Mark ONE candidate
4
Senator
🟨 Yellow — Form 28
Vote for your county Senator. They represent your county in the Senate and safeguard devolution funds and county rights.
✓ Mark ONE candidate
5
Governor
🟦 Blue — Form 29
Vote for your county Governor & Deputy Governor as a pair. They run county services — health, roads, agriculture and local infrastructure.
✓ Mark ONE candidate pair
6
Ward MCA
🟫 Beige — Form 25
Vote for your Ward MCA — your most local rep. They sit in the county assembly and control the Ward Development Fund (WDF) for your immediate community.
✓ Mark ONE candidate
At the Polling Station — Step by Step
1
Arrive with your original National ID or Passport. Photocopies are not accepted.
2
Queue at your specific stream — assigned by ID number. Check the notice board outside.
3
The presiding officer verifies your ID and fingerprint. Your name is ticked off the register.
4
You receive 6 ballot papers — one per election. Confirm you have all 6 before entering the booth.
5
In the private booth, place ONE mark on each ballot beside your chosen candidate. A tick ✓, cross ✗, or thumbprint — all are valid.
6
Fold each ballot from left to right through the centre to hide your vote, then drop it into the correct box.
7
Your finger is marked with indelible ink to prevent double voting. Polls close at 5:00 PM — if you are in the queue by 5 PM you will be allowed to vote.
Any of These Marks = Valid Vote
✓Tick
✗Cross
👍Thumbprint
·Any mark
⚠️ Spoilt ballot: If you mark more than one candidate on a ballot, or write anything else on the paper, that vote will not be counted. It does NOT affect your other 5 ballots.
